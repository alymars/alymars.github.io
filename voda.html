Designed with the needs and safety of sailors, surfers, diver, and watersport aficionados in mind, Voda is a responsive web application that provides an easy way to understand wind, wave, and weather forecasts, and safety reporting.

  • Duration: March - November 2022
  • Platform: Web
  • Tools Used: Figma, UsabilityHub, Proto.io, ProtoPie, Optimal Workshop, Google Survey
  • What I Did: Competitive analysis, User surveys, User interviews, Wireframing, Prototyping, Visual design, A/B testing, Usability testing

The Process

To better understand what information is important when designing a weather application, I conducted a competitors analysis. I focused on applications with a similar target market, people who do water sport. The oppurtunities for improvement I found were providing activity recommendations, tracking algae blooms, and offering safety suggestions. Along with the analysis, I noted navigation menus and how data is displayed could be simplified.

Early iterations of Voda included having each forecast detail on its own card/page, along with a pop-up window for viewing safety information, and a 2-3 sentenced overview of the forecast. During usability testing, this layout was not favortable. Participants found overview text redundant and would have preferred the safety information to replace it instead of a pop-up window. This layout was also not efficient once I began to add the 10-Day and Monthly view. So I had to rethink my design and find another way to provide all the necessary information without overwhelming the user. Voda would go through five design iterations before deciding on the final design.

The design is meant to be minimalist and professional, while still feeling warm and friendly. Voda is designed to be fully navigational in as few click as possible. The menu only contains the essentials; weather favorite locations, settings, help, and legal information. On the forecast page there is an easy to navigate sub menu that shows the forecast views and the radar. All information is displayed on the same screen and is concise to avoid excessive scrolling.

Conclusion

Testers of the final product found the application appealing and intuitive. They were able to move through the app with little to no heaistation. This was the first application I fully designed from concept to high-fidelity prototypes. I learnt how much truly goes into making a good app, and how proper researching and testing up front saves and frustration.
